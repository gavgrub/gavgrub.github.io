Impartial Predjudice

A project built for the Hack the Bias 2026 hackathon. The hackathon had the theme of developing software to combat bias in a given area of society. My group (not taking the prompt seriously) decided that instead of reduce bias, we should develop software to maximize bias, arguing that it would display where bias exists online. As a result, Impartial Predjudice was born.

Using automated interaction on TikTok, it prompts the user for a target concept (dogs, music, politics) and proceeds to scroll on TikTok, analyzing video metadata and visuals with AI, scoring each video's relevance, and selectively engaging with aligned content. Over time, this skews the feed towards or away from a single viewpoint, showing how opposing "noise" is gradually silenced. The project highlights the mechanisms behind algorithmic bias and has implications for education, research, and policy.

Spirits of Steel: A New Dawn

A game heavily inspired by Paradox Interactive's Hearts of Iron IV, SOSAND was my main project during my final year of high school, and my first semester of university. It allows users to govern any country across a range of historical scenarios and attempt to prepare for a world war. The game has a number of systems, including military, industry, and political, which allows for hours of replayability

It is the sequel to my game Spirits of Steel, but vastly expanded.

The game was extremely sucessful, with 70k downloads, an active community, and featured by YouTubers such as Drew Durnil.

Unfortunately, I have since stopped developing the game. Being developed so early into my education, the game's architecture is not scalable, which makes the project frustrating to work on. Moreover, game development is not a focus of mine anymore. However, the community keeps it alive in their various discord servers and subreddits.

Watershed Visualizer

The first website I developed, made during my internship at South East Alberta Watershed Alliance. It helps visualize waterflow in the South Saskatchewan River Sub-Basin, a watershed extending from Alberta to Saskatchewan.

Display options include river flow, major settlements, reservoirs. You can also click on rivers to check it's wikipedia page, or see the outline of either the SSRSB or SEAWA area.
