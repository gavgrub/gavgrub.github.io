Computer Science Undergraduate Society

The Computer Science Undergraduate Society (CSUS) is a social club at the University of Calgary for those interested in computer science. It provides a space for students to engage in academic and social events, and operates a study / hangout room.

CSUS hosts the largest undergraduate hackathon in Calgary, Calgary Hacks. In 2026, the event had 414 participants, producing 31 submissions.

As (Co) Vice President Academic, I collaborate with faculty and the other execs to run events supporting student learning.

In 2026, my key contributions involved hosting a LeetCode workshop, a review session for CPSC 251 (Theoretical Foundations of Computer Science I), and a Git workshop for SENG 300 (Introduction to Software Engineering). The later two were organized in collaboration with their respective course coordinators.

Schulich Soundstage

Schulich Soundstage is the largest music club at the University of Calgary. The club is responsible for hosting music events such as open mics, jam sessions, and a music festival. Additionally, it offers gig opportunities, workshops, and a radio show on CJSW.

As Vice President Logistics, I manage club transactions, funding applications, and queing forms during open mic nights.

I hold two other roles in the club. Firstly, on it's web development team, where I help maintain it's website. Secondly, on the club's gig roster, where I perform guitar and bass at events such as the uCalgary open house or uCalgary strong festival.
